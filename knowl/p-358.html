Complex numbers are of the form \(z = a+bi\) where \(a,b\in\mathbb{R}\) and \(i=\sqrt{-1}\text{.}\) Thus \(i^{2}=-1\text{.}\) Complex numbers have a polar representation \(z = r e^{i\theta}\text{,}\) where \(r = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}\) and \(\theta = \arctan\frac{y}{x}\text{.}\) In the polar representation, we should think about \(r\) as a radius and \(e^{i\theta}\) as a point on the unit circle.
