Find the value of \(b\) for which the given equation is exact, and then solve it using that \(b\text{:}\) \(\left(xy^{2}+bx^{2}y\right)dx+\left(x+y\right)x^{2}dy\)
  1. If this equation is exact then \(M_{y}=N_{x}\text{,}\)
    \begin{align*} M_{y} \amp =2xy+bx^{2}\\ N_{x} \amp =3x^{2}+2yx \end{align*}
    and are only equal when \(b=3\text{.}\) Follow the arrows until close the diagram:
    \begin{align*} \text{Start here:} \amp \psi_{x}=xy^{2}+3x^{2}y \amp \implies \amp \psi=\int\left(xy^{2}+3x^{2}y\right)dx+h(y)\\ \amp \amp \amp \boldsymbol{\psi=\frac{1}{2}x^{2}y^{2}+x^{3}y+h(y)}\\ \amp \amp \amp \Downarrow\\ \amp \psi_{y}=x^{3}+x^{2}y \amp \Longleftarrow \amp \psi_{y}=x^{2}y+x^{3}+h^{\prime}(y) \end{align*}
  2. Solve for \(h(y)\) by noting that since
    \begin{align*} x^{3}+x^{2}y=x^{2}y+x^{3}+h^{\prime}(y)\implies \amp h^{\prime}(y)=0\\ \implies \amp h(y)=C \end{align*}
  3. Put it all together and get \(\psi(x,y)=\frac{1}{2}x^{2}y^{2}+x^{3}y+C\) and hence the implicit solution is \(\frac{1}{2}x^{2}y^{2}+x^{3}y=C.\)
