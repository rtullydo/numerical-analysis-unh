When the forcing function of an undamped system is far from the natural frequency of a undamped system, forcing function and the natural behavior of the system interact in a combination of constructive and destructive interference.

However, when the frequency of the forcing function approaches the natural frequency of the system, constructive and destructive interference tend to cluster into locally strong or weak oscillations called beats.

\begin{equation*} \frac{\omega_{0}}{2\pi}\approx\frac{\omega}{2\pi}\iff\omega_{0}\approx\omega. \end{equation*}
