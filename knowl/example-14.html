Example 2.2.6.

Solve the IVP

\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dt}=t^{4}y\,\,\,\,\,\,y(0)=1. \end{equation*}

  • Solution:

  • Start with equilibrium solutions \(y=0\text{.}\)

  • Get \(\left|y\right|=Ce^{t^{5}/5}\) but notice that by choise of \(C\) this shortens to \(y=Ce^{t^{5}/5}\text{.}\)

  • Note that this includes the equilibrum solution \(y=0\) by setting \(C=0\text{.}\)

  • Thus then general solution is given by

    \begin{equation*} y_g(t)=Ce^{t^{5}/5}. \end{equation*}

  • To solve the IVP we use the initial condition

    \begin{equation*} 1=y(0)=Ce^{0}=C \end{equation*}

    thus \(C=1\text{,}\) hence the particular solution to the IVP is

    \begin{equation*} y_p(t)=e^{t^{5}/5}. \end{equation*}
