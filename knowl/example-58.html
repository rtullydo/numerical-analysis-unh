Example 3.4.4.

Suppose we get that the general solution comes out to

\begin{equation*} y(t)=c_{1}e^{3t}\cos t+c_{2}e^{3t}\sin t. \end{equation*}

Then just remember when finding constants corresponding to initial conditions that we need to use product rule to find the derivative of \(y(t)\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} y^{\prime}(t)=3c_{1}e^{3t}\cos t-c_{1}e^{3t}\sin t+3c_{2}e^{3t}\sin t+c_{2}e^{3t}\cos t. \end{equation*}
