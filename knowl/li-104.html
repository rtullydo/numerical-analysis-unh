Step 4: Find the common denominator and solve using separation of variables.

Write

\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dt}=6-\frac{y}{12}=\frac{72-y}{12} \end{equation*}

and using separation of variables we get

\begin{align*} \frac{dy}{dt}=\frac{72-y}{12} \amp \iff \amp \frac{dy}{72-y}=\frac{dt}{12}\\ \amp \iff \amp -\ln\left|72-y\right|=\frac{t}{12}+C_{1}\\ \amp \iff \amp \ln\left|72-y\right|=\frac{-t}{12}+C_{2}\\ \amp \iff \amp \left|72-y\right|=C_{3}e^{-\frac{t}{12}}\\ \amp \iff \amp 72-y=ke^{-\frac{t}{12}}\\ \amp \iff \amp y=72-ke^{-\frac{t}{12}}. \end{align*}

Solving the IVP by using \(y(0)=5\text{,}\) we get

\begin{align*} y(0)=5 \amp \iff \amp 72-ke^{0}=5\\ \amp \iff \amp k=72-5=67 \end{align*}

so the final solution is

\begin{equation*} y(t)=72-67e^{-\frac{t}{12}}. \end{equation*}
