Let's find the particular solution to the IVP:

\begin{equation*} y^{\prime\prime}+9y=0,\,\,\,\,\,\,y(0)=-2,\,\,\,y^{\prime}(0)=1 \end{equation*}

Step 1: We can jump straight to the characteristic equation:

\begin{equation*} r^{2}+9=0 \end{equation*}

and get \(r=\pm3i\text{.}\)

Step 2: The general solution is

\begin{align*} y(t) \amp =c_{1}e^{0t}\cos3t+c_{2}e^{0t}\sin3t.\\ \amp =c_{1}\cos3t+c_{2}\sin3t. \end{align*}

Step 3: Using the initial conditions \(y(0)=-2,\,\,\,y^{\prime}(0)=1\) we need to first take a derivative

\begin{align*} y(t) \amp =c_{1}\cos3t+c_{2}\sin3t\\ y^{\prime}(t) \amp =-3c_{1}\sin3t+3c_{2}\cos3t \end{align*}

hence

\begin{align*} -2 \amp =y(0)=c_{1}+0\\ 1 \amp =y^{\prime}(0)=0+3c_{2} \end{align*}

so that

\begin{equation*} c_{1}=-2,c_{2}=\frac{1}{3}. \end{equation*}

Thus the solution is

\begin{equation*} y(t)=-2\cos3t+\frac{1}{3}\sin3t. \end{equation*}
