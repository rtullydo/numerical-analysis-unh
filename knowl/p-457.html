We consider equations of the form

\begin{equation*} mu^{\prime\prime}+\gamma u^{\prime}+ku=F(t) \end{equation*}

where \(m>0,\gamma>0,k>0\) are mass, damping coefficients, and spring constant. Here \(F(t)\) represents external force done to the mass-spring system. (e.g wind or cars driving on a bridge) While these forcing functions come in many forms, one of the most important cases is where the forcing function is itself a harmonic oscillation (you can think of this as corresponding to an ambient vibration).
