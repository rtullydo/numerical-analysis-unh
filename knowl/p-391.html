Consider the corresponding homogeneous equation

\begin{equation*} y^{\prime\prime}+p(t)y^{\prime}+q(t)y=0,. \end{equation*}

whose general solution we'll call \(y_{h}\text{.}\) Now suppose that the particular solution \(y_p(t)\) solves the nonhomogeneous equation. Then \(y_p + y_h\) also solves the equation, since

\begin{align*} \amp(y_p + y_h)'' + p(y_p + y_h)' + q(y_p + y_h)\\ \amp= y_p'' + py_p' +q y_p + y_h'' +p y_h' + qy_h \\ \amp= g(t) + 0 \\ \amp= g(t). \end{align*}
