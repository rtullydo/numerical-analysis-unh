Find the general solution for

\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{dt}=y^{2}. \end{equation*}

  • First let's find the equilibrium solutions: \(y(t)=0\) is the only one.

  • Then use the general separation of variables procedure

    \begin{align*} \frac{dy}{dt}=y^{2} \amp \iff \amp \frac{1}{y^{2}}dy=dt\\ \amp \iff \amp \int\frac{1}{y^{2}}dy=\int dt\\ \amp \iff \amp -\frac{1}{y}=t+C. \end{align*}

  • But notice that

    \begin{equation*} y=-\frac{1}{t+C} \end{equation*}

    does NOT solve the IVP with \(y(0)=0\text{.}\) Thus we have to include the equilibrium solution \(y(t)=0\) to get the complete general solution. In this case we say the general solution is:

    \begin{equation*} y_g(t) = \begin{cases} 0\\ -\frac{1}{t + C} \end{cases} \end{equation*}

Moral of the story: Always find the equilibrium solutions first in case there are any missing solutions from separating variables!
